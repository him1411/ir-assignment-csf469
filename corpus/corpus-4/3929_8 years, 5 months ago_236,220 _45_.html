In the merge commit this file will be created in your branch also.When you merge your branch back into the trunk, SVN tries to do the same again: It sees that a file was created in your branch, and tries to create it in your trunk in the merge commit, but it already exists! This creates a tree conflict.The way to avoid this, is to do a special merge, a reintegration. You can achieve this with the --reintegrate switch.You can read about this in the documentation:\nhttp://svnbook.red-bean.com/en/1.7/svn.branchmerge.basicmerging.html#svn.branchemerge.basicmerging.reintegrateWhen merging your branch back to the trunk, however, the underlying\n mathematics are quite different. Your feature branch is now a mishmash\n of both duplicated trunk changes and private branch changes, so\n there\'s no simple contiguous range of revisions to copy over. By\n specifying the --reintegrate option, you\'re asking Subversion to\n carefully replicate only those changes unique to your branch. (And in\n fact, it does this by comparing the latest trunk tree with the latest\n branch tree: the resulting difference is exactly your branch changes!)After reintegrating a branch it is highly advisable to remove it, otherwise you will keep getting treeconflicts whenever you merge in the other direction: from the trunk to your branch.
