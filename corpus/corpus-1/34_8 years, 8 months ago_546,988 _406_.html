When we say for two functions f and g, f = O(g) it means that f <= g. However, this does not mean that for any n f(n) <= g(n). Actually what it means is that f is less than or equal g in terms of growth. It means that after a point f(n) <= c*g(n) if c is a constant. And after a point means than for all n >= n0 where n0 is another constant.
