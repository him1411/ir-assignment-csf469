The general practice in python is that, if the property is likely to be there most of the time, simply call it and either let the exception propagate, or trap it with a try/except block. This will likely be faster than hasattr. If the property is likely to not be there most of the time, or you\'re not sure, using hasattr will probably be faster than repeatedly falling into an exception block.As Jarret Hardie answered, hasattr will do the trick.
