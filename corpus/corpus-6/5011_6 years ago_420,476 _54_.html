As Ceki pointed out, there were tons of jars that WebSphere was using and one of them was pointing to an older version of slf4j. The No-Op rollback happens only with slf4j -1.6+ so anything older than that will throw an exception and halts your deployment.There is a documentation in SLf4J site which resolves this. I followed that and added slf4j-simple-1.6.1.jar to my application along with slf4j-api-1.6.1.jar which I already had. This solved my issue.
