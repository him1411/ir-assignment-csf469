How do you know whether a variable has been set at a particular place in the code at runtime? This is not always obvious because (1) the variable could be conditionally set, and (2) the variable could be conditionally deleted. It\'s cleaner and clearer to write:For this particular case it\'s better to do a = None instead of del a. This will decrement reference count to object a was (if any) assigned to and won\'t fail when a is not defined. Note, that del statement doesn\'t call destructor of an object directly, but unbind it from variable.
