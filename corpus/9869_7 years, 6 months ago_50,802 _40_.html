When the main body of code becomes much too weighty to change easily, you find yourself having to pick small parts of it, define interfaces for them, and then allowing people to change behaviour via the objects that plug into those interfaces. That\'s all well and good, but it\'s better to avoid that sort of complexity in the first place.It\'s also the symptom of a statically-typed language.
